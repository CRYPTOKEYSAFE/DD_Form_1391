Subject: Camp Schwab FY26 Block 9 — Proposed PAX Coding Change (5-Building FSRM Portfolio)

Rob,

I went into the PAX 1391 Processor and walked through how Block 9 is coded today for the Camp Schwab FY26 portfolio (SCH-1024, SCH-3213, SCH-3237, SCH-3270, SCH-3314), then sat with the May 2024 MCON/MCNR Consistency Review Board Guidelines and the printed 1391 to see where the current entry sits relative to what the manual prescribes for line-item structure. SCH-1024 is the worked example. Three attachments to this email: the CRB Guidelines manual, a short Word-doc walkthrough that steps through the SCH-1024 entry, and a one-page data-flow diagram that maps each input field to where it lands on the printed form.

Two notes up front. The change is in draft only — nothing has been entered or modified in PAX yet, since we are in a pencils-down editing moratorium for the FY26 cycle. And the dollar values do not change; Total Funded Cost and Total Project Cost stay consistent with the cost estimate workbook to the dollar.

Here is what's in PAX today for SCH-1024. The Block 9 Data items table holds 18 rows: the iNFADS-locked building line at the top (MULTI PURPOSE BEQ/BOQ/CO HQS, Quantity 1.00, Unit Cost $10,412,000.00), eleven scope rows entered as dollar items with UNIFORMAT II Level 2 prefixes (B20, C10, C30, D20, D30, D40, D50, E20, F20, G10, G20), and six more dollar rows for the rollup adders (Contingency $552K, LSH $2,413K, Planning and Design $509K, SIOH $679K, CM $339K, DB Design $401K). The Associated Costs dialog has four percent slots — Contingency, Supervision Inspection and Overhead, Design Build, and Planning and Design — and all four are presently set to 0.00%. With the rates at zero, PAX renders Subtotal $10,412K, Contingency (0.0%) $0, SIOH (0.0%) $0, Total Funded Cost $10,412K, Total Project Cost $10,412K, Planning and Design (0%) (NON ADD) $0 on the printed form. The math is correct because every adder is already absorbed into a dollar line item.

The proposed change moves the rollup adders out of the items table and into the Associated Costs dialog where they belong as percentages. After the change the items table holds scope only — the iNFADS-locked building line plus the eleven scope rows with the UNIFORMAT prefixes stripped and the descriptions rewritten in plain English. The Associated Costs dialog is populated with the four supported rates: Contingency 10.00%, Supervision Inspection and Overhead 8.00%, Design Build 4.00%, and Planning and Design 10.00%. The Planning and Design slot covers the NAVFAC 6% Design plus 4% Construction Management combined, since PAX has no native CM percent slot; the breakdown is disclosed in the Block 10 narrative. The walkthrough doc has the renamed-line table and a data-flow diagram showing where each input lands on the printed form.

LSH is the one wrinkle. PAX's Associated Costs dialog has no Life Safety and Health slot, so the $2,413K (2.5% of iNFADS PRV $96,523,347 per NAVFAC 11010.44E CH-1 / MCO 11000.12) cannot ride under a percent. Two options preserve the dollar value: keep LSH as a single dollar line item in the items table (the current method, simplest path), or bake LSH into the scope total with the breakout disclosed in Block 10. Either works. The methodology call goes to Bil before PAX entry.

One verification step at entry. PAX's internal base for each Associated Costs percentage has not been observed outside the present all-zero state, so the exact resulting Total Project Cost cannot be predicted without rendering it. The workbook target is $10,413K rounded ($10,012K Total Funded). After the change, the printed form gets generated and reconciled against the workbook; if a delta shows up, resolution is one of: switch the LSH option, tune the percentage rates to match PAX's base, or align the workbook to PAX's calculation. The reconciliation gets logged in Section F-11 of the cost estimate workbook.

If the proposed coding reads cleanly for SCH-1024, the same approach mirrors across the other four buildings (the percentage rates are identical for all five; the LSH option chosen for SCH-1024 applies to the other four). Available for a quick walk-through whenever works for you.
